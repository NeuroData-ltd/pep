Core principles

dilisense stand for three core principles that are detailed below.

1. Publicly accessible data

Our primary data source is always the official website of a data subject (for example the parliament's website of a specific country). If this is not available for any reason, we include other public sources to enable an independent PEP verification in case of a positive match. 2. Holistic financial risk screening

We don't believe in a plain "the more the better"-mentality, but in a risk-based approach of Financial Risk Screening Programs.

A holistic financial risk screening program comprises of three areas

Screening logic

Companies need to define CDD and EDD processes that suit their business context and geographical area. Some of the questions to be addressed are: - What PEP types are relevant for the business and for which countries? - What events trigger the screening of what PEP type (e.g. exceptional high transaction volumes, suspicious frequencies)? - What Corruption Perception Index have the countries of my customers?

Screening data

The quality of the input data and the screening result correlate with each other. Customer data should at least consist of the full name, the country and the date of birth. Additional data is helpful in case the name is quite common and would potentially result in many hits. As almost every hit triggers a manual verification process in the Compliance team, there is an inherent interest of avoiding false positives due to poor data quality.

Screening provider

Screening providers need to ensure High data quality: All available data needs to be provided in a unique and searchable format. Sufficient data coverage: The relevant PEP types for the relevant countries need to be covered. Suitable screening technology: Options for filtering (e.g. by date of birth or gender) as well as for fuzzy search have to be available.

For further details on a risk-based approach, we recommend reading the following article published by ACAMS: Drowning in PEPs.
